Conduct & Standards

How you show up — on the water, on shore, and with students, parents, and colleagues — matters every single day.

Communication & Conduct

  • Stay current with staff WhatsApp updates — check it regularly
  • Use radios professionally — short, clear, and respectful. Refer to the Radio Etiquette Guide in the Staff Hub
  • Be on time for setup and stay through takedown — every shift
  • Phones only for emergencies or weather/tides check — no scrolling during program time
  • No music on the water
  • Maintain a clean, professional appearance — uniform on, tidy boats and gear

Respectful Interactions

  • Be friendly and welcoming with students, parents, and guests at all times
  • Use inclusive, age-appropriate language with every group
  • Avoid gossip, venting, or unprofessional conversations near students or parents
  • If a customer is upset: stay calm, listen briefly, then pass to a manager
  • Conflicts with other staff? Bring it to your manager respectfully and privately

Accountability & Leadership

  • Report all incidents — big or small. Use the correct logs and tell a lead or manager
  • Witness bullying, unsafe behaviour, or inappropriate language? Speak up immediately to a senior or manager
  • Use downtime to help the team, tidy up, or prepare — not for phone breaks
  • Be open to feedback — it's how you grow
  • Be proactive — offer to help before being asked

Progressive Discipline System

New in 2026. This system ensures fairness, accountability, and clear expectations for everyone.

1st Write-Up — Verbal Warning

Conversation with supervisor. Issue documented internally. Staff member loses 50% of bonus eligibility.

2nd Write-Up — Written Warning

Formal written documentation. Clear expectations for improvement outlined. Staff member loses 100% of bonus eligibility.

3rd Write-Up — Dismissal

Termination of employment.

Module 4

Safety

You help shape the safety culture for your students and co-workers. This is the most important thing you do.

⚠️
Always ask before launching: "Is this safe?" Every single time, without exception.

Before Every Session

  • Everyone wearing a properly fitted lifejacket
  • Everyone dressed for the conditions
  • Headcount completed — all students accounted for
  • Sailboats rigged correctly — no tangles in sheets, plugs in
  • Coach boat outboard mounted tight and secured
  • Wind, tides, and weather assessed
  • Radio on you — on, loud, and ready

During the Session

  • Complete headcounts frequently throughout the session
  • Monitor energy levels — hydration, fatigue, overheating
  • Keep a radio on you at all times. Use clear, professional calls
  • If wind picks up: slow the launch, reduce boat numbers, reef sails, consider rides
  • Turn engine OFF when near people in the water — drift in or use a paddle

When Something Goes Wrong

1

Immediate danger?

Call for help on the radio immediately and act. Do not wait. Do not hesitate.

2

Secure the group

Make sure the rest of the class is in a controlled environment, supervised by another coach.

3

Stay with the problem

Don't leave until the situation is resolved. Needing backup is not a failure — it's the right call.

4

Report & debrief

Fill out an incident report. Debrief with your team. Communicate with parents — symptoms can be delayed. Tell your manager immediately.

Risk Mitigation

  • Proper rigging — no tangles in sheets, plugs in, correct boom height
  • Manage boat balance — number of people per boat, size of participants
  • Be aware of speed and other boats while driving a coach boat
  • If wind is too strong: slow the launch, reduce boats, reef sails, consider rides
  • Not sure? Talk to a senior instructor or manager before making a call

Module 5

Coach Boats

Coach boats are safety tools. Every procedure exists for a reason — follow them without exception.

Before Launching

  • Ensure both outboard clamps are tight before launching
  • Use the designated safety tie line — always check it is properly tied
  • A loose outboard can fall off or damage the boat — ask a lead if unsure
  • Minimum gear on board: lifejacket, paddle, bailer, whistle
  • Lifejacket must be worn at all times
  • Fuel tank must be vented and strapped down properly
  • Radio check before leaving the dock

Kill Cord

⚡
Kill cord must be worn at all times when the engine is on. Clip on before starting the engine. Remove only after the engine is shut off. Not wearing a kill cord is a severe safety hazard and a possible cause for a write-up.

Engine Near Swimmers

  • Engine must be turned off when near people in the water
  • Drift in or use a paddle for final approach
  • Never reverse near swimmers
  • Brief passengers if approaching a group in the water

Tilting the Mercury Outboard

  • Turn off engine
  • Ensure tilt lock lever is in the unlocked (down) position
  • Shift engine into forward gear
  • Lift engine up nearly all the way until you hear the final "click"
  • Release and gently press down — engine should stay in place
  • Move the tilt lock lever into the locked (up) position
  • To lower: shift to neutral, unlock the lever, support the engine, lower smoothly
  • Never run the motor tilted up. Always tilt up before hauling on dolly.
  • Ask a lead if unsure — don't force anything

A Typical Day

Great sessions don't happen by accident — they follow four clear phases every time.

Every smooth, professional session follows the same four phases. Know them, follow them, and help your senior keep the class on track.

1

🗓️ Planning — Arrive Early

Check conditions (wind, tide, weather). Review group size, age, and skill level. Confirm staffing and coach boat assignments. Prep radios, gear, and lesson materials. Assign roles. Review goals and safety plan with your team. Set the course or drills before launching — a coach should be doing this while you organize life jackets and wetsuits.

2

🚀 Launching — Controlled & Organized

Senior hits the water early to set the course. Juniors organize students, assign boats, review shore skills. Bring your radio — on, loud, and ready. On Day 1: demo who sits where, tiller use, rudder, centreboard, tacking basics. Launch boats one at a time after they complete a lap. Use radios to manage flow and keep support on the beach.

3

⛵ On the Water — Coach Actively

Keep the group together and make sure everyone understands the course and objective. Run skill-based drills — one clear skill at a time. Move from boat to boat giving individual feedback. Match your coaching to the drill — keep it targeted and specific. Use positive reinforcement, calm tone, and clear corrections. Watch for fatigue, safety issues, and disengagement.

4

📋 Debrief — Don't Skip It

Do not skip the debrief — ever. It gets students into a pattern and allows for individual and group feedback. Leave them with a positive message and something to look forward to so they're stoked when they head home. Complete logs as a team at the end of every session.

Sign-Out & Dismissal

  • Organize your students and staff — this is your responsibility
  • Make sure kids are leaving with the right person on time
  • Stay on top of packing up — don't let it drag
  • Have students clean up garbage and personal items
  • Dismissal is often when you interact with parents — be warm, specific, and positive

Talking to Parents

👨‍👩‍👧
Upset parent at pickup? Stay calm → listen briefly → pass to a manager. Never argue or over-explain.
💬
Student behaviour issue? Pull the parent aside privately. Lead with something positive, then be specific and factual about the issue. Loop in your senior before the conversation if it's serious.

Hands On → Hands Off

  • Do it for them at first — by the end of the week they should be more independent
  • Guidance, not full control. Give them ownership as they progress
  • By midweek, step back and let them lead more — you're building confidence, not dependency

CANSail & Checklick

Know the system so you can support your senior and contribute to student progression tracking.

CANSail Logs

  • At the end of every class the senior leads a quick instructor debrief using the log
  • Remind your senior to do this — it's a great learning opportunity for you
  • Covers: what happened in class, any student issues, boat repairs to log, and what to plan for next day
  • Logs mean another instructor can pick up the class if someone is sick
  • Log QR code is on the office cupboard door

Checklick

  • Mid-course: review checklists with the senior and talk to any students not on track
  • Thursdays end of day (or Saturdays of second weekend for weekend classes) — senior uploads checklists
  • Students receive an email from Checklick when uploaded — they may need to check junk mail
  • Issues logging in: tell them to email info@macsailing.com
  • All youth students are in Checklick. Adults need to have paid the $20 Sail Canada registration fee
  • Have the progress conversation before uploading — their level should never be a surprise

CANSail 2.0 Updates

  • Core skills for each level have not changed
  • New standardized methods for consistency across the country
  • Checklists now use "developing / performing / extending" — only need "performing" checked to complete
  • "Extending" is for students exceeding the standard — it's a bonus, not a requirement
  • In-depth Instructor Packs with lesson plans, diagrams, and drills are in the Staff Hub — read them
📱
Checklick login: app.checklick.com — confirm your login before your first class. Search by "year class code" e.g. 2026 AC10.
